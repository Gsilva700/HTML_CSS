Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.

The man who dreamed of equality and justice for all

Timeline of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.'s Life:

  • 1929 - Born in Atlanta, Georgia, USA.
  • 1944 - Graduates from Booker T. Washington High School and enters Morehouse College at the age of 15.
  • 1948 - Earns a Bachelor of Arts degree in Sociology from Morehouse College.
  • 1951 - Graduates with a Bachelor of Divinity degree from Crozer Theological Seminary.
  • 1953 - Marries Coretta Scott in Marion, Alabama.
  • 1954 - Becomes pastor of Dexter Avenue Baptist Church in Montgomery, Alabama.
  • 1955 - Leads the Montgomery Bus Boycott after Rosa Parks' arrest, a pivotal event in the Civil Rights Movement.
  • 1957 - Founds the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC) to fight for civil rights.
  • 1963 - Delivers his iconic "I Have a Dream" speech during the March on Washington.
  • 1964 - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his nonviolent struggle against racial inequality.
  • 1965 - Leads the Selma to Montgomery marches to advocate for voting rights.
  • 1968 - Assassinated on April 4 in Memphis, Tennessee, at the age of 39.
